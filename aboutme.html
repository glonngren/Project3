ABOUT ME

Introduction

My name is George Lonngren, I am 27 years old and I live in the West suburbs of Chicago in a town called Lombard, Illinois. I was born and raised in the West Suburbs and graduated High School from Willowbrook HS in Villa Park, IL. Through out High School, I had kept a promise to myself that I would serve in the military after witnessing the attacks on 9/11 in New York, Pennsylvania and Washington D.C. I graduated high school early to join the United States Marine Corps.

In 2009 I enlisted as a Communications Equipment Repairer. I've fixed almost any type of communications gear that plugs into a wall, from XBOX 360s to rotary telephones. After serving five years in the Marines, I was honorably discharged and became a field engineer in the oil fields of Oklahoma. I was personally involved in the construction of over one-hundred wells. After oil prices dropped, I was laid off and made the decision to go to college, and thats where I am today. I hope to earn a bachelor's degree in Information Technology Management with a focus in software development.

Programming

I got my start in programming in the Marine Corps when a project was started to develop automated test routines that would trouble-shoot UHF (ultra high frequency) radios and decrease turn around time in the maintenance cycle. I was personally tasked to create a basic configuration test that would set up a CISCO digital VOIP switch and test whether it could handle a fully configured digital network of phones. Utilizing Python, I developed a test-routine and basic GUI to allow Marines to test digital switches and decrease the maintenance cycle time by weeks. This accomplishment developed in me an interest in programming and my desire to become a software engineer is still burning within me to this day.
